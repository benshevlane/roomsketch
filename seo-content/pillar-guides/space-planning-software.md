# Space Planning Software: The Best Free Tools for Homeowners

There's no shortage of space planning software out there — from free browser tools to professional-grade applications that cost hundreds of pounds a year. For most homeowners, the right tool is the simplest one that does the job well. This guide compares the main options honestly and helps you choose the right one for your project.

Spoiler: for the vast majority of home planning projects, [Free Room Planner](https://freeroomplanner.com) — free, browser-based, no sign-up — is the right choice. But read on to understand why, and when other tools might be a better fit.

---

## What to Look for in Space Planning Software

For home use, the ideal space planning tool should:

- Let you enter real room measurements and see them accurately reflected
- Have a furniture library with standard household pieces at realistic dimensions
- Allow you to drag, drop, rotate, and resize items easily
- Work on your device without a complex installation
- Cost nothing or very little for personal use

Professional tools offer more: 3D visualisation, rendering, BIM integration, detailed material libraries. These are impressive features, but they come with a steep learning curve and substantial cost. For planning where to put your sofa, they're overkill.

**Purpose:** Planning rooms with IKEA furniture
**Cost:** Free

**Furniture library:** IKEA products only, at accurate product dimensions

### RoomSketcher — Best for 3D Visualisation

**Purpose:** General room planning with 3D visualisation
**Cost:** Free (limited) or subscription (approximately £49/year for full features)
**Platform:** Browser and desktop app
**Furniture library:** Extensive
**Learning curve:** Moderate
**Best for:** People who want to see their room in 3D, or projects where visual presentation matters
**Limitation:** The free version is quite restricted; the features that make it worthwhile require a subscription

RoomSketcher's main advantage over Free Room Planner is its 3D rendering capability. If you want to visualise how your room will look with new paint colours, specific furniture, or a particular layout before committing, the 3D views are genuinely useful. ### SketchUp Free — Best for Technical Users

**Purpose:** Professional-grade 3D modelling
**Cost:** Free (web version) or paid (desktop)
**Platform:** Browser-based (free version)
**Learning curve:** Steep — designed for professionals and technically minded users
**Best for:** Technically inclined homeowners planning complex projects, or anyone who wants to learn 3D modelling for future use
**Limitation:** Significant time investment to get useful results; unnecessary complexity for most home planning projects

SketchUp is a genuinely powerful tool, but it's designed for architects and designers. For most homeowners, the learning curve is simply not worth it for a one-time room planning project. If you're technically minded and enjoy learning new software, it's free — but be prepared for a learning investment before you get productive results.

### HomeByMe — Best for Full Home Planning

**Purpose:** General room and home planning with 3D
**Cost:** Free (limited) or subscription
**Platform:** Browser-based
**Furniture library:** Extensive, including some branded furniture
**Learning curve:** Moderate
**Best for:** Full home layout planning where you want to visualise multiple rooms in context
**Limitation:** Free tier has credit limits on renders; can feel complex for simple room planning tasks

HomeByMe is useful for planning an entire home rather than individual rooms — you can see how all your rooms connect and how choices in one room affect the look and feel of adjacent spaces. For multi-room renovation projects, this broader view can be valuable.

### Planner 5D — Best for AR Features

**Purpose:** Room planning with 3D and AR (augmented reality)
**Cost:** Free (limited) or subscription
**Platform:** Browser and mobile app
**Learning curve:** Low to moderate
**Best for:** Users who want to use AR to visualise furniture in their actual room via their phone camera
**Limitation:** Most useful features require a subscription; AR works better in larger rooms

Planner 5D's AR feature lets you point your phone camera at your room and see virtual furniture placed in the actual space. It's a genuinely novel feature that can be useful for checking whether a specific piece of furniture will look right in proportion. But it's not a substitute for accurate planning with real measurements.

## Which Software Is Right for Your Project?

### Quick furniture rearrangement or single room layout

Use [Free Room Planner](https://freeroomplanner.com). It takes 20–30 minutes to plan a room accurately and costs nothing. No learning curve, no sign-up, no download.

### Full kitchen or bathroom renovation

Use Free Room Planner for the overall layout, then use the manufacturer's own planner (most major kitchen brands have one) for detailed unit placement. Many fitters will do a free CAD plan as part of their quote process.

### Buying a lot of IKEA furniture

Use the IKEA Home Planner alongside Free Room Planner. IKEA's tool gives you accurate product dimensions; Free Room Planner lets you check the whole room including non-IKEA pieces.

### Full home renovation or extension

For major structural projects, you'll eventually need professionally drawn plans. Use Free Room Planner or RoomSketcher for early concept planning and conversations with your architect; leave the formal plans to the professionals.

### Presenting to a contractor or partner

A screenshot from Free Room Planner with annotations works for most contractor conversations. For more impressive visual presentations, RoomSketcher's 3D views or HomeByMe's room renders can be useful.

## Free vs Paid Space Planning Software

For home use, free tools are almost always sufficient. The main things paid tools add are: higher-quality 3D rendering; larger furniture libraries with branded products; ability to export high-resolution plans; collaboration features. For a homeowner planning their own rooms, none of these are necessary.

The only scenario where a paid subscription makes clear sense for personal use is if you're renovating the whole house and want high-quality 3D visuals to help you make decisions about materials and finishes — the 3D helps you visualise the end result more accurately than a top-down plan.

## Space Planning Software FAQ

### Is there a completely free space planning tool?

Yes. [Free Room Planner](https://freeroomplanner.com) is completely free — no subscription, no account, no download. It works in any browser and has everything you need to plan any room in your home.

### Do I need 3D space planning software?

For most planning decisions — does this furniture fit, does the layout work, are the clearances right — a 2D top-down plan is all you need. 3D is useful for visualising materials, colours, and the overall feel of a room, but it's not necessary for functional planning.

### Can space planning software replace an interior designer?

For layout decisions, largely yes — a good tool and the principles in this guide will take you a long way. For colour, material, and styling decisions, an interior designer's trained eye adds real value. For major structural or renovation projects, professional input is important.

### How much does professional space planning software cost?

Professional tools like AutoCAD cost several hundred pounds per year. RoomSketcher is approximately £49 per year. Most professional tools offer free trials. For personal home planning, free tools like Free Room Planner are entirely sufficient.
