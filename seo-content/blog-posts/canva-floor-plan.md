# Canva Floor Plan: Can You Use Canva for Room Planning?

Canva is one of the most popular free design tools on the internet, and it makes sense that people ask whether it can be used to create a floor plan. The short answer: Canva can help you create a visual representation of a floor plan, but it's not built for accurate room planning. Here's an honest look at what it can and can't do — and when a purpose-built room planner is a better choice.

## What Canva Can Do for Floor Plans

Canva does have floor plan capabilities. It has a set of floor plan templates and a basic library of furniture shapes that you can drag onto a canvas, resize, label, and arrange into something that looks like a floor plan.

Canva works well for:

- **Creating a presentation-quality visual** of an already-designed layout — for example, if you want to present a floor plan concept to a client, an estate agent, or a contractor in a polished document
- **Making a quick schematic diagram** of a space for a mood board or design brief
- **Adding text labels, annotations, and branding** to an existing floor plan image
- **Sharing a floor plan concept** as part of a marketing document, PDF, or proposal

---

## What Canva Can't Do for Room Planning

Canva is a graphic design tool. It's not a room planning tool. The key limitations when you actually need to plan a room:

**No true-to-scale functionality.** This is the fundamental problem. Canva doesn't work in real-world units. You can't enter that your room is 4.5m x 3.8m and have it reflected accurately. Sizing is visual, not dimensional. Whatever you draw will look like a room, but it won't tell you whether your actual sofa actually fits.

**No furniture library with real dimensions.** Canva's furniture shapes are generic illustrative drawings, not dimensionally accurate objects. A "sofa" in Canva is a decorative element. You can't resize it to 215cm x 87cm and have that mean anything in relation to your room.

**No snap-to-wall or accurate grid alignment.** Canva has grid guides, but they're not designed for the precise placement you need to check whether a wardrobe fits in an alcove or a bed clears a door swing.

**No door swing visualisation.** One of the most important practical planning features — marking which way a door opens and ensuring nothing sits within the swing arc — isn't built into Canva's floor plan capabilities at all.

The result of all of this: floor plans made in Canva look good, but they can't tell you whether your sofa actually fits in your room. And for most people planning a room, that's the entire point.
