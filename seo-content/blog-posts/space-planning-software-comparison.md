There's no shortage of space planning software — from free browser tools to professional applications that cost hundreds of pounds a year. For most homeowners, the right tool is the simplest one that does the job well. Here's an honest comparison of the main options so you can choose the right one for your project.

For most home planning projects, the answer is [Free Room Planner](https://freeroomplanner.com) — free, browser-based, no sign-up, works on any device. But read on to understand when other tools might suit you better.

---

## What Homeowners Actually Need From Space Planning Software

The requirements are simpler than the market makes them appear. A good home planning tool should:

- Accept real room measurements and reflect them accurately
- Have a furniture library with standard household pieces at realistic dimensions
- Allow easy drag, drop, rotate, and resize operations
- Work without a complex installation or expensive subscription
- Produce results you can act on — i.e., tell you whether something actually fits

Professional tools add 3D rendering, advanced material libraries, BIM integration, and collaboration features. These are powerful capabilities for design professionals. For planning where to put your sofa, they're significant overkill.

### IKEA Home Planner — Best for IKEA Purchases

**Cost:** Free
**Platform:** Browser-based
**Furniture library:** IKEA products only, at accurate product dimensions
**Best for:** Projects where you're buying primarily from IKEA
**Limitation:** Useless for non-IKEA furniture — use alongside Free Room Planner for mixed rooms

IKEA's tool is excellent for what it does. Planning a full IKEA kitchen or PAX wardrobe system with it gives you accurate product dimensions and lets you see exactly what will fit. For anything outside the IKEA catalogue, it offers nothing. Use both tools together for any room that mixes IKEA with other furniture.

### RoomSketcher — Best for 3D Visualisation

**Cost:** Free (limited) or approximately £49/year (full)
**Platform:** Browser and desktop app
**Learning curve:** Moderate
**Best for:** People who want to see their room in 3D before committing
**Limitation:** The free version is quite limited; the features worth having require a subscription

RoomSketcher's main advantage is its 3D rendering. If you're making significant decisions about materials, colours, or the overall look and feel of a renovation and want to visualise the end result before committing, the 3D views are genuinely useful. The subscription cost is reasonable if you're doing a major project.

### SketchUp Free — Best for Technical Users

**Cost:** Free (web version)
**Platform:** Browser-based
**Learning curve:** Steep
**Best for:** Technically inclined homeowners who want professional-grade results
**Limitation:** Significant time investment required; not practical for most homeowners

SketchUp is a professional tool that requires professional amounts of time to learn. If you enjoy software and have a complex project, it's worth the investment. If you want a working room plan this afternoon, it's entirely the wrong choice.

### HomeByMe — Best for Whole-Home Planning

**Cost:** Free (limited) or subscription
**Platform:** Browser-based
**Best for:** Planning multiple rooms in context with each other
**Limitation:** Can feel complex for simple single-room tasks; free tier has credit limits

HomeByMe is useful when you need to see how rooms relate to each other — useful for whole-home renovation planning. For a single room, Free Room Planner is simpler and just as effective.

## Which Tool Is Right for Your Project?

- **Quick furniture rearrangement or single room layout:** Free Room Planner. 20–30 minutes, costs nothing, no learning curve.
- **Planning a kitchen or bathroom renovation:** Free Room Planner for the overall layout, then the manufacturer's own planner for detailed unit sizing.
