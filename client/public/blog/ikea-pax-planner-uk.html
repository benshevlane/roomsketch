Planning Your IKEA PAX Wardrobe in the UK: Everything You Need to Know

If you've ever stood in the wardrobe section of an IKEA store with a tape measure in one hand and a catalogue in the other, you'll know exactly how overwhelming it can be. The IKEA PAX system is one of the most popular wardrobe solutions in the UK, and for good reason — it's modular, affordable, and endlessly customisable. But getting the planning right before you buy is absolutely essential, and that's where a good room planner becomes your best friend.

In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about using the IKEA PAX Planner UK tool, what its limitations are, and how a free browser-based floor planner like freeroomplanner.com can help you visualise your entire bedroom layout — not just the wardrobes — before spending a single penny.

What Is the IKEA PAX System?

The PAX wardrobe system from IKEA is a range of modular wardrobe frames and interiors that can be combined to create a fully bespoke storage solution. Whether you need a single wardrobe in a box room or a full wall of fitted storage in a master bedroom, the PAX range has a configuration to suit almost every UK home.

Key Features of the PAX System

  • Frame sizes: PAX frames come in widths of 50cm, 75cm, and 100cm, and heights of 201cm and 236cm
  • Depths: Standard depth is 58cm, with a 35cm depth option for smaller spaces
  • Doors: Choose from hinged, sliding, or open configurations
  • Interiors: Mix and match shelves, drawers, trouser rails, shoe racks, and more
  • Finishes: Available in white, black-brown, oak effect, and other finishes

Because the system is modular, the planning stage is crucial. Getting the measurements wrong — even by a few centimetres — can mean your PAX frames don't fit the wall, or worse, you end up with an awkward gap that looks untidy and wastes space.

Using the IKEA PAX Planner UK Tool

IKEA offers its own online planning tool on its UK website, which is specifically designed for the PAX system. The IKEA PAX Planner UK lets you enter your room dimensions, choose your frame sizes, select door styles, and even pick interior fittings. It's a genuinely useful starting point and gives you a 3D preview of your chosen configuration.

How to Use the Official IKEA PAX Planner

  1. Go to the IKEA UK website and navigate to the PAX wardrobe planner section
  2. Enter the width and height of the wall where your wardrobe will sit
  3. Choose your frame widths to fill the space (a combination of 50cm, 75cm, and 100cm units)
  4. Select a door style — sliding doors require a minimum of two frames side by side
  5. Customise the interior with shelves, drawers, and rails using the IKEA KOMPLEMENT range
  6. Save or export your design and add the items directly to your IKEA shopping list

Limitations of the IKEA PAX Planner

While the official tool is excellent for configuring the wardrobes themselves, it does have some notable limitations — particularly for homeowners who are planning a full bedroom renovation:

  • It only shows the wardrobes, not the wider room context
  • You can't see how the wardrobes will interact with the bed, dressing table, or other furniture
  • It doesn't account for door swing clearance or walking space around the bed
  • It won't show you whether sliding doors will be obstructed by a radiator or window
  • There's no way to plan multiple rooms or export a full floor plan

This is where a dedicated floor planner becomes invaluable — and why thousands of UK homeowners use freeroomplanner.com alongside the IKEA PAX Planner UK to get a complete picture of their bedroom layout.

Tips for Getting Your PAX Wardrobe Measurement Right

Before you start planning — whether using the IKEA PAX Planner UK or a floor plan tool — accurate measurements are everything. Here are some essential tips:

Measure Twice, Buy Once

  • Measure the wall width at floor level, at mid-height, and at ceiling level — walls in older UK homes are often not perfectly straight
  • Note any skirting boards that might affect the base of the wardrobe
  • Check the ceiling height carefully — the 236cm PAX frames are designed for rooms with higher ceilings
  • Measure the depth available, especially if the wardrobe will be near a radiator or light switch

Account for the Ceiling

If you want your PAX wardrobes to go all the way to the ceiling for a built-in look, you'll need to add a PAX filler piece to bridge the gap between the top of the frame and the ceiling. Factor this into your planning and measure the gap carefully.

Consider the Floor

PAX frames are designed to sit directly on the floor. If you have thick carpet, this can affect stability and the appearance of the finished unit. Some fitters recommend removing carpet beneath the frames, or using adjustable feet — worth discussing with your fitter before you begin.

Alternatives to PAX: When to Consider a Custom Build

PAX wardrobes are a fantastic solution for most UK bedrooms, but they're not always the right choice. Here are some situations where a custom-built wardrobe might serve you better:

  • Rooms with sloped ceilings or unusual angles (loft conversions, for example)
  • Alcoves that aren't a standard PAX width — no combination of 50cm, 75cm, and 100cm frames fits perfectly
  • Very deep alcoves where the standard 58cm depth doesn't make the most of the space
  • Period properties where the aesthetics of a flat-pack wardrobe don't suit the room's character
