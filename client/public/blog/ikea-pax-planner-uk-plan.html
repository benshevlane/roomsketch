Planning Your Perfect PAX Wardrobe in the UK

If you've ever spent an afternoon on the IKEA website trying to figure out how your dream wardrobe will actually fit into your bedroom, you're not alone. The IKEA PAX planner UK is one of the most popular online design tools for British homeowners — and for good reason. PAX wardrobes are modular, affordable, and endlessly customisable. But planning them effectively takes a bit of know-how, and often the official planner only tells half the story.

This guide walks you through everything you need to know about planning a PAX wardrobe in the UK — from using IKEA's own tool to understanding how a free room planner like freeroomplanner.com can help you see the bigger picture before you spend a single penny.

What Is the IKEA PAX Wardrobe System?

PAX is IKEA's flagship wardrobe range. It's a modular system built around standard frames that you can combine, stack, and fit with a huge variety of internal organisers, doors, and accessories. In the UK, PAX frames come in three heights (201cm, 226cm, and 236cm) and several widths (50cm, 75cm, and 100cm), with a standard depth of 58cm or a slimmer 35cm option.

The beauty of the PAX system is its flexibility. You can create anything from a single wardrobe in a box room to a full walk-in wardrobe arrangement across an entire wall. The internal fittings — from KOMPLEMENT shelves and pull-out trays to shoe racks and tie organisers — allow you to tailor the interior to your exact storage needs.

Why PAX Is So Popular in UK Homes

  • Modular sizing: PAX frames slot together seamlessly, making it easy to fill an alcove or cover a full wall.
  • Wide price range: You can keep costs low with basic frames and simple interiors, or invest more in soft-close hinges and premium organisers.
  • Flat-pack delivery: Available for home delivery or click-and-collect from IKEA UK stores, including Edinburgh, Wembley, Birmingham, and more.
  • DIY-friendly: Most homeowners can assemble PAX wardrobes without professional help, though larger installations benefit from two people.
  • Design flexibility: Dozens of door styles and colours mean PAX can suit both modern and traditional interiors.

How the IKEA PAX Planner UK Works

The official IKEA PAX planner UK is a browser-based tool available directly on the IKEA website. It allows you to build a virtual wardrobe by selecting frames, adding doors, and choosing internal fittings. Here's a quick overview of how it works:

  1. Set your space: Enter the width of the wall or alcove where your wardrobe will go. The planner will suggest frame combinations that fit.
  2. Choose your frames: Select the width, height, and depth of each PAX frame. You can combine multiple frames side by side.
  3. Add doors: Pick from sliding or hinged door options, choosing your preferred style, colour, and handle.
  4. Configure the interior: Add shelves, drawers, clothes rails, shoe organisers, and other KOMPLEMENT fittings to each frame.
  5. Review and add to basket: The planner generates a complete shopping list that you can add directly to your IKEA basket.

It's a solid tool for designing the wardrobe itself — but it has one notable limitation: it shows your wardrobe in isolation, not in the context of your actual room.

The Limitation of Planning a Wardrobe in Isolation

Here's the problem many UK homeowners run into: they spend hours perfecting their PAX configuration, order everything, and then discover that the wardrobe — while perfectly planned in itself — creates unexpected problems in the room. Common issues include:

  • Wardrobe doors clashing with radiators or bedroom doors when opened
  • Not enough clearance to walk comfortably around the bed once the wardrobe is in place
  • Blocking natural light from windows
  • Misjudging how much floor space remains for other furniture
  • Failing to account for skirting boards or coving that affects how flush the wardrobe sits against the wall

This is exactly where a dedicated room planner comes in. Before you finalise your PAX design, it's worth stepping back and looking at your entire bedroom layout.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your PAX Wardrobe Plan

Measure Twice, Order Once

Before you touch the IKEA PAX planner UK tool, grab a tape measure and note down your room's key dimensions. You'll want:

  • The total width of the wall where the wardrobe will go
  • The ceiling height (and whether there's a coving that reduces usable height)
  • The distance from the wardrobe wall to the opposite wall or nearest obstacle
  • The position of sockets, light switches, and radiators
  • The swing arc of any nearby doors

Feed these measurements into your freeroomplanner.com floor plan first, then use the IKEA planner to design the wardrobe itself.

Consider the Depth Carefully

The standard PAX depth is 58cm. In a small UK bedroom — many of which are under 3 metres wide — this can feel significant. Add 58cm of wardrobe depth to a 135cm double bed width, and you're left with very little room to manoeuvre. The slimmer 35cm PAX depth is worth considering in tighter spaces, particularly for rooms where the wardrobe will face the foot of the bed.

Think About Door Type Before You Design the Interior

Sliding doors and hinged doors each have implications for the internal layout. Sliding doors, for example, mean you can never fully open both sections of the wardrobe at the same time, which affects how you organise items inside. Hinged doors require clearance space in front — typically 50–60cm — so they're less practical if the bed is positioned close to the wardrobe. Settle on your door type before finalising the internal configuration.

Don't Forget Lighting

IKEA offers integrated LED lighting solutions for PAX wardrobes. If you're planning a larger wardrobe installation, particularly a walk-in arrangement, lighting makes a significant practical difference. The free bedroom planner can help you visualise whether there's enough natural light reaching your wardrobe area or whether supplementary lighting will be needed.

Planning a Fitted Wardrobe Look with PAX

One of the most popular uses of the PAX system in the UK is creating a fitted wardrobe aesthetic without the cost of bespoke joinery. By extending PAX frames to the ceiling with filler panels and painting the frames and doors to match your room's colour scheme, many homeowners achieve a built-in look for a fraction of the price.

To plan this effectively, your floor plan needs to show the wardrobe running wall to wall. Draw your room in the bedroom planner, then size your wardrobe block to span the full width of the alcove or wall. This helps you see exactly how the fitted look will interact with the rest of your furniture arrangement and whether anything will need to move.

When to Involve a Professional

For most straightforward PAX installations, confident DIYers can manage independently. However, there are situations where bringing in a professional makes sense:

  • Walk-in wardrobes: If you're creating a multi-wall or full room wardrobe setup, a kitchen or wardrobe fitter can ensure frames are level, secure, and well-finished.
  • Electrical work: Adding integrated lighting that requires new wiring should always be handled by a qualified electrician.
  • Structural considerations: If you're removing an internal wall to create a dressing room or walk-in wardrobe space, you'll need a structural assessment.
  • Sharing plans with a fitter: If you are working with a contractor, you can export your freeroomplanner.com floor plan as a PNG and share it with them to communicate your vision clearly — no specialist software required on their end.

Is the IKEA PAX planner available in the UK?

What's the difference between using the IKEA PAX planner and freeroomplanner.com?

How do I work out how many PAX frames I need?

Measure the width of the wall or alcove where your wardrobe will go. PAX frames come in 50cm, 75cm, and 100cm widths. Choose a combination of frames that adds up to slightly less than your available wall width, leaving a small gap you can fill with panels. For example, a 250cm wall could take two 100cm frames and one 50cm frame. The IKEA PAX planner will suggest combinations automatically once you input your wall width.
