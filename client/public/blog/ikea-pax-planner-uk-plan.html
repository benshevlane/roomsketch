Planning Your IKEA PAX Wardrobe: Everything UK Homeowners Need to Know

If you're redesigning your bedroom and considering an IKEA PAX wardrobe system, you're in good company. The PAX range is one of the most popular wardrobe solutions in the UK, thanks to its modular design, affordable price points, and the sheer number of configuration options available. But before you head to the IKEA website or visit your nearest store, there's one crucial step that many homeowners skip — properly planning the surrounding bedroom layout first.

The IKEA PAX planner UK tool on the IKEA website is useful for configuring the wardrobe itself, but it doesn't show you how the wardrobe fits within your actual bedroom. Will the doors clear your bed frame? Is there enough floor space to open drawers fully? Can you still access the window without squeezing past a wardrobe unit? These are questions that a dedicated room planner can answer long before you spend a penny.

That's where freeroomplanner.com comes in. It's a completely free, browser-based floor planner that lets you draw your room to scale, place furniture including wardrobe units, and check measurements in metres, centimetres, or feet — all without signing up or downloading anything. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to plan your PAX installation effectively, what to measure, and how to get the most from both tools.

What Is the IKEA PAX System and Why Is Planning So Important?

The IKEA PAX system is a customisable wardrobe framework. You choose your frame dimensions (width, depth, and height), then fill it with internal fittings — shelves, drawers, hanging rails, shoe racks, and more. Doors can be hinged or sliding, and the finish options range from white and grey to wood-effect panels.

Available frame widths in the UK are typically 50cm, 75cm, and 100cm, with heights of 201cm or 236cm, and depths of 35cm or 58cm. This modularity is what makes PAX so appealing — you can combine multiple units to fill an entire wall — but it's also what makes planning essential.

Common Mistakes Without Proper Planning

  • Underestimating door swing clearance — hinged doors need significant clearance in front of them. If your bed sits close to the wardrobe, hinged doors may not open fully.
  • Forgetting skirting boards — PAX units don't always sit flush against the wall if skirting boards are in the way. This affects how the unit looks and how stable it feels.
  • Not accounting for the ceiling height — the 236cm height option is great for filling vertical space, but it's only suitable if your ceiling is high enough and the room can handle the proportions.
  • Buying the wrong number of units — without measuring your wall precisely and mapping it out, it's easy to order too few or too many frame sections.
  • Ignoring traffic flow — a wardrobe that technically fits but blocks the natural path through the room will be frustrating to live with every day.

Using the IKEA PAX Planner UK Tool

IKEA's own online PAX planner (found on the IKEA UK website) is specifically designed to help you configure the inside of your wardrobe. You can select your frame dimensions, choose internal fittings, and get a price estimate. It's a genuinely helpful tool for deciding what goes inside the unit.

However, the IKEA PAX planner UK tool has its limitations. It doesn't let you place your wardrobe within a scale drawing of your actual room. You can't see how it interacts with your bed, your door, or your window. And it doesn't account for the rest of your bedroom furniture.

This is why professional bedroom designers and savvy homeowners use a separate room planner alongside IKEA's own tool — one that gives them the full picture of the space.

PAX Installation Tips for UK Bedrooms

UK homes present some specific challenges when it comes to installing large wardrobe systems like PAX. Here are some things to keep in mind as you plan:

Alcoves and Chimney Breasts

Many UK homes — particularly Victorian and Edwardian terraces — have alcoves on either side of a chimney breast in the bedroom. These can be perfect for PAX units, but they require precise measurement. The alcove width needs to be a multiple of available PAX frame widths, or you'll need to fill the gap with a filler panel. Use the room planner to draw your alcove accurately and test different PAX width combinations.

Sloped Ceilings in Loft Conversions

If your bedroom is in a loft conversion, the sloped ceiling makes PAX planning more complex. The taller 236cm units may only be possible in the centre of the room where the ceiling is highest. Sketch this out carefully in your floor plan and always measure the ceiling height at the point where the back of the unit will stand.

Sliding vs. Hinged Doors

For smaller UK bedrooms, PAX sliding doors are often a better choice because they don't require clearance in front of the wardrobe to swing open. However, sliding doors do mean you can only access half the wardrobe at a time. In your room plan, consider which door type works best given the other furniture positions.

Fitting to the Full Wall

Many UK homeowners want their PAX units to span an entire wall for a built-in look. To achieve this, you'll need to combine multiple frame widths and potentially use filler strips or panels at the ends. Measure your wall precisely — including any irregularities — and use your room plan to confirm the total wardrobe width before ordering.

What PAX dimensions are available in the UK and how do I represent them in the room planner?

In the UK, PAX frames are available in widths of 50cm, 75cm, and 100cm. Depths are either 35cm or 58cm, and heights are 201cm or 236cm. In Free Room Planner, you can drag and resize any furniture item to match these exact dimensions. How do I plan a floor-to-ceiling PAX wardrobe in a UK bedroom?

First, measure your ceiling height accurately. If you're using the 236cm PAX frames, you'll need a ceiling height of at least 240cm to comfortably fit the units (allowing for fixing to the top panel). Draw your room in Free Room Planner with accurate wall heights in mind, position your wardrobe units along the intended wall, and check that no other furniture conflicts with the space. For angled ceilings in loft conversions, measure at the exact point where each unit will stand.

Can I share my room plan with an IKEA kitchen or bedroom specialist?
