Planning Your IKEA PAX Wardrobe in the UK: Everything You Need to Know

If you're thinking about fitting an IKEA PAX wardrobe into your bedroom, you're certainly not alone. The PAX system is one of the most popular wardrobe solutions in the UK, loved by homeowners for its modularity, affordability, and the sheer number of customisation options available. But before you head to your nearest IKEA store or place an order online, there's a crucial step that too many people skip: planning your space properly.

Whether you're working with a compact box room, a spacious master bedroom, or an awkward alcove, getting your measurements right and visualising how your PAX units will fit into the room can save you time, money, and a great deal of frustration. This guide walks you through how to use the IKEA PAX planner UK tool effectively, what to consider when designing your wardrobe layout, and how a free browser-based floor planner can help you see the bigger picture before you commit to anything.

What Is the IKEA PAX Planner?

The IKEA PAX planner is a dedicated online configurator available on the IKEA UK website. It allows you to build a virtual version of your PAX wardrobe by selecting frame sizes, door styles, interior fittings, and finishes. You can experiment with combinations of frames — typically in widths of 50cm, 75cm, and 100cm, and heights of 201cm and 236cm — to create a wardrobe that suits your storage needs.

The IKEA PAX planner UK version is tailored to products available in the UK market, with prices displayed in pounds sterling and dimensions in centimetres. Once you're happy with your design, you can save it, share it, or add the items directly to your shopping list.

What the PAX Planner Does Well

  • Product accuracy: Every item in the configurator matches exactly what's available to buy in the UK.
  • Interior customisation: You can add shelves, drawers, trouser racks, pull-out rails, and more within each frame.
  • Door selection: Choose from sliding or hinged doors in a wide range of finishes, including mirrored options.
  • Pricing in real time: As you build, the total cost updates so you can manage your budget.
  • Save and share: Designs can be saved to your IKEA account and shared with others.

What the PAX Planner Doesn't Do

Here's where things get interesting. The IKEA PAX planner is excellent for designing the wardrobe itself, but it doesn't help you plan the room around it. It won't show you whether your bedroom door will still open freely, whether there's enough floor space to walk around your bed, or how the wardrobe will interact with other furniture in the room. That's a significant limitation — and it's one that a proper floor planning tool addresses directly.

Why You Should Plan Your Whole Room, Not Just the Wardrobe

Imagine ordering a full wall of PAX units, having them delivered, and then realising the configuration blocks natural light from your bedroom window, or that the sliding doors need more clearance than you accounted for. These are real scenarios that happen to real homeowners every year.

Planning your entire bedroom layout before committing to a wardrobe purchase is simply good sense. A floor plan lets you:

  • Check that the wardrobe fits the wall space without obstructing doors, windows, or radiators.
  • Ensure there's comfortable clearance in front of sliding or hinged wardrobe doors.
  • Visualise how the wardrobe relates to other furniture like your bed, chest of drawers, and dressing table.
  • Confirm the proportions feel right before spending hundreds of pounds on flat-pack furniture.

This is where freeroomplanner.com comes in. It's a free, browser-based floor planner that requires no sign-up, no download, and no account. You draw your room walls using a snap-to-grid system (accurate to 10cm), add furniture items, and see live measurements in metres, centimetres, or feet — whichever you prefer.

  1. Measure your room carefully. Use a tape measure to record the length and width of the room, plus the position and width of doors, windows, and any alcoves or recesses. Note the height of the ceiling too — this matters when choosing between 201cm and 236cm PAX frames.
  2. Draw your room in Free Room Planner. Head to freeroomplanner.com and use the wall drawing tool to create an accurate floor plan. The snap-to-grid feature makes it easy to input precise measurements. Use the bedroom planner for a room-specific experience.

PAX Wardrobe Sizes: What Fits Where

Understanding the available PAX frame dimensions is essential when planning your wardrobe. Here's a quick reference for the UK range:

Frame Widths

  • 50cm wide — ideal for narrow alcoves or as an end unit.
  • 75cm wide — a versatile middle-ground option.
  • 100cm wide — the widest single frame; perfect for maximising storage.

Frame Heights

  • 201cm — standard height, suitable for rooms with ceilings of at least 220cm.
  • 236cm — taller option, great for rooms with high ceilings and for creating a more built-in look when paired with a cornice filler.

Frame Depth

  • 58cm — standard depth; requires at least 58–60cm of clear floor space in front of hinged doors, and more for comfort.
  • 35cm — shallow depth; useful in tight hallways or small bedrooms where every centimetre counts.

When you lay these dimensions out in your floor plan on Free Room Planner, you can immediately see whether a single 100cm frame, a pair of 75cm frames, or a full wall of mixed frames is the right call for your bedroom.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your PAX Planning

Think About Door Clearance First

This is the number one mistake people make. Hinged PAX doors require the full width of the door to swing out freely — for a 100cm frame, that means you need 100cm of clear space in front of the wardrobe. Sliding doors require no swing clearance but do need the door to overlap with the adjacent frame, which means you can't access both halves simultaneously.

Consider Built-In Fillers for a Custom Look

If your wall is slightly wider than a neat combination of PAX frames, IKEA sells filler strips that bridge the gap. Alternatively, you can plan around this by leaving a small gap at one end and adding a shelf or hook unit. Mapping this out on your floor plan first will save you from awkward gaps on installation day.

Account for Skirting Boards

Standard PAX frames don't sit flush to the floor if you have tall skirting boards. You may need to cut away the skirting or use filler pieces at the base. Measure your skirting height and factor it into your planning — particularly if you're going for a truly built-in look.

Plan the Interior Fittings Around Your Wardrobe Habits

The IKEA PAX planner UK tool lets you add internal fittings like the KOMPLEMENT range of shelves, drawers, and rails. Think about who's using the wardrobe and what they're storing. A mix of hanging space, shelving, and drawer units tends to work well for most households. Plan these inside the configurator once you've locked in the external dimensions from your room floor plan.
