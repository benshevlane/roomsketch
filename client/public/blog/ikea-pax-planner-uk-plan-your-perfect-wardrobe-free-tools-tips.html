Planning Your IKEA PAX Wardrobe: Everything UK Homeowners Need to Know

What Is the IKEA PAX Planner?

The IKEA PAX planner is an online tool provided by IKEA that allows you to configure PAX wardrobe frames, doors, and interior fittings. Available through the IKEA UK website, it lets you mix and match:

  • Frame sizes (widths of 50cm, 75cm, and 100cm; heights of 201cm and 236cm)
  • Door styles (sliding, hinged, mirrored, and more)
  • Interior organisers (drawers, rails, shelves, shoe racks, and pull-out trays)
  • Finishes and colours (white, oak effect, black-brown, and others)

Once you've built your configuration, the ikea pax planner uk tool gives you a shopping list and rough cost estimate. It's a genuinely useful starting point — but it only shows you the wardrobe in isolation, not how it will sit within your actual bedroom.

The Limitation of Wardrobe-Only Planning

Here's the problem that catches many UK homeowners out: a wardrobe that looks perfect on screen can become a nightmare in a real room. Common issues include:

  • Door swing clearance — hinged PAX doors need up to 60cm of clearance in front of them. If your bed or chest of drawers is too close, the doors simply won't open fully.
  • Alcove fit — many UK bedrooms have chimney breast alcoves or awkward recesses. A PAX unit might look like it fits, but without accurate room measurements, you could end up 5cm short or 10cm too wide.
  • Traffic flow — can you still walk comfortably around the bed once the wardrobe is installed? Is there enough space to open all the drawers?
  • Ceiling height and coving — older UK homes often have decorative coving that reduces usable wall height. The taller 236cm PAX frames may not fit without modification.

This is exactly where a proper room planning tool becomes essential — and where freeroomplanner.com fills the gap that the ikea pax planner uk tool leaves open.

Understanding PAX Dimensions: A Quick UK Reference

Before you start planning, it helps to have PAX dimensions to hand. Here are the standard frame sizes available through IKEA UK:

Frame Widths

  • 50cm wide — ideal for tighter spaces or filling gaps
  • 75cm wide — the most versatile size for standard bedrooms
  • 100cm wide — great for maximising storage, but check your wall space

Frame Heights

  • 201cm — suits most standard UK ceiling heights (typically 240cm), leaving room for coving
  • 236cm — better for rooms with higher ceilings; may need a gap filler or ceiling trim for a built-in look

Frame Depth

  • 35cm or 58cm — the deeper option accommodates hanging clothes more comfortably and is the most popular choice

When you draw your wardrobe on freeroomplanner.com, use these exact dimensions so your floor plan reflects what you'll actually be installing.

Tips for Getting Your PAX Wardrobe Right First Time

Planning a PAX wardrobe in a UK home comes with its own set of quirks. Here are some practical tips based on common challenges:

Measure Your Room Accurately

Use a laser measure or steel tape measure — not a fabric tape — for the most accurate results. Measure each wall at least twice, and also check that your walls are square (they often aren't in older UK properties). Enter these measurements into your freeroomplanner.com floor plan as your starting point.

Account for Skirting Boards

PAX frames don't sit flush against a wall if there are skirting boards in the way. Standard UK skirting boards are typically 1.5cm to 2cm deep. You can either cut back the skirting where the wardrobe will sit, or factor this into your available wall space by subtracting the skirting depth from your wall measurement.

Think About the Lighting

PAX wardrobes can feel dark inside, especially in a bedroom that doesn't get a lot of natural light. IKEA sells internal LED strip lights that fit the PAX system — factor these into your budget, and consider whether a nearby plug socket will be accessible once the wardrobe is in place.

Plan Your Interior Storage Before You Buy

The beauty of the PAX system is its interior flexibility, but it can also be its downfall if you don't plan it properly. Before configuring drawers and shelves in the IKEA PAX planner UK, make a list of exactly what you need to store — long dresses, folded jumpers, shoes, suits — and work backwards from there to select the right fittings.

Consider a Built-In Look

Many UK homeowners want their PAX to look like bespoke fitted furniture rather than flat-pack. This can be achieved with infill panels, crown moulding at the top, and careful painting of the surrounding walls. Planning this in advance — including how you'll handle gaps and edges — makes the installation process much smoother.

When to Involve a Professional

The DIY route works well for most PAX installations, but there are situations where bringing in a professional makes sense:

  • If your bedroom has very uneven floors or ceilings that need levelling before installation
  • If you're planning an integrated lighting setup that requires new electrical work
  • If you want a truly bespoke built-in look with carpentry work around the unit
  • If you're working with an unusual alcove shape that requires custom modifications

In these cases, a contractor or bedroom fitter will often ask you for a room plan before they visit. Exporting your freeroomplanner.com floor plan as a PNG gives them exactly what they need to understand your space and quote accurately — saving time for everyone involved.
