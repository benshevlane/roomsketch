  • Shareable plans: Export your layout as a PNG image and share it with your bathroom fitter, kitchen maker, or contractor instantly.

Common Bathroom Layout Challenges — and How Planning Ahead Solves Them

Even experienced bathroom fitters encounter layout challenges. For homeowners tackling their first renovation, these issues can come as a nasty surprise if you haven't planned properly. Here are some of the most common problems — and how using bathroom design software helps you avoid them.

Not Enough Clearance Around Fixtures

Building regulations and general usability guidelines recommend minimum clearance distances around bathroom fixtures. For example, there should ideally be at least 70cm of clear space in front of a toilet or basin. If you're working in a small bathroom, these clearances can quickly eat into your available space. Drawing your layout to scale lets you see potential clearance issues before you commit to a purchase.

Plumbing Constraints

While software won't move your soil stack for you, having a scaled layout helps your plumber or bathroom fitter quickly assess what's feasible. You can clearly mark the position of existing waste pipes and water supplies, and explore layouts that minimise the need for expensive plumbing changes.

Door and Window Conflicts

It's surprisingly easy to plan a beautiful bathroom layout only to realise the door swings into the toilet, or that a new vanity unit blocks the window. Because the planner lets you draw the full room outline — including door swings and window positions — you can spot these conflicts at the planning stage.

Underestimating Storage Needs

Bathrooms are notorious for lacking storage. When you can see your full layout on screen, it's easier to identify wall space where shelving, cabinets, or a mirrored bathroom cabinet could be added without cluttering the room.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Bathroom Layout Plan

Here are a few practical tips to help you create the most useful and accurate bathroom plan possible:

  • Measure twice. Before you start drawing, measure your bathroom carefully — including the positions of doors, windows, and any existing plumbing outlets. The more accurate your input, the more useful your plan will be.
  • Use real product dimensions. When adding fixtures to your plan, resize them to match the exact dimensions of the products you're considering buying. Most bathroom suppliers list dimensions clearly on their product pages.
  • Plan for ventilation. Don't forget to mark the position of your extractor fan or window when planning. Building regulations require adequate ventilation in UK bathrooms, and this can influence where certain fixtures are placed.
  • Consider the wet zone. In shower areas, think about splash zones and where tiles or waterproof panels will need to be applied. Your layout plan is a great reference document for your tiler.
  • Share early and often. Export your plan early and share it with your bathroom fitter, plumber, and tiler. Getting their input at the planning stage — rather than when work has already started — can save significant time and money.
