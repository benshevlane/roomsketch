Standard B&Q Kitchen Unit Sizes: What You Need to Know

Before you start planning, it helps to understand the standard dimensions of B&Q kitchen units. Most are designed to follow the UK standard kitchen module sizes, which makes mixing units from a single range straightforward.

Base Units

Base units are the lower cupboards that sit on the floor and support your worktop. Standard heights and depths are fairly consistent across ranges:

  • Height: Typically 720mm (excluding legs and worktop)
  • Depth: Usually 570mm (to allow for a 600mm worktop with a slight overhang)
  • Widths: Common widths include 300mm, 400mm, 500mm, 600mm, 800mm, and 1000mm

Wall Units

Wall units are the upper cupboards mounted above the worktop:

  • Height: Available in 360mm, 540mm, 600mm, and 720mm
  • Depth: Typically 300mm
  • Widths: Match base unit widths — 300mm, 400mm, 500mm, 600mm

Tall Units

Tall units are full-height cupboards used for pantry storage, integrated ovens, or housing a larder fridge:

  • Height: Usually 1970mm or 2150mm
  • Depth: 570mm (same as base units)
  • Widths: Typically 300mm, 500mm, or 600mm

How to Measure Your Kitchen Before Planning

Accurate measurements are the foundation of any good kitchen plan. Even a 50mm error can throw off your entire layout. Here's a simple step-by-step measuring guide:

  1. Measure the overall room dimensions: Length and width of each wall, from corner to corner.
  2. Note window and door positions: Measure the distance from each corner to the edge of windows and doors, plus their widths.
  3. Record window sill heights: This affects whether you can fit wall units above a window or a sink below it.
  4. Mark fixed points: Note the position of radiators, soil pipes, boiler, gas supply points, and electrical sockets. These can all affect where units can go.
  5. Measure ceiling height: Especially important if you're considering tall units or ceiling-height wall units.

The Most Popular Kitchen Layout Types for B&Q Units

B&Q kitchen units are designed to work with all the standard kitchen layout configurations. Here are the most common layouts and how to approach planning each one:

Galley Kitchen

Two rows of units facing each other along parallel walls. Common in smaller homes and flats. Works well with B&Q's narrower unit widths. Key consideration: ensure at least 1200mm of clearance between facing units so two people can pass each other comfortably.

L-Shaped Kitchen

Units run along two adjacent walls forming an L shape. This is one of the most versatile layouts and suits mid-size kitchens well. A corner base unit (often a carousel or pull-out unit from B&Q) is needed where the two runs of units meet.

U-Shaped Kitchen

Units on three walls. Maximises storage and worktop space. Needs careful corner unit planning on both internal corners. Requires a larger room — typically at least 2.4 metres wide to allow comfortable movement.

Island Kitchen

A freestanding island unit in the centre of the kitchen. B&Q offers island units in several sizes. You'll need at least 1 metre of clearance on all sides of the island. Use our kitchen floor plan tool to check if your room is large enough before committing.

Single-Wall Kitchen

All units along one wall only. Typical in open-plan spaces or studio flats. Simple to plan but storage can be limited — tall units become very important here.

Planning Tips for Specific Kitchen Areas

The Kitchen Triangle

The classic design principle of the "kitchen work triangle" — the path between your hob, sink, and fridge — still holds up. When planning your B&Q kitchen units, try to keep these three points within easy reach of each other without overlapping walkways. A total triangle perimeter of between 4 and 8 metres is considered ideal.

Sink Positioning

Your sink needs to be positioned near existing plumbing (unless you're having the pipework moved). In most UK kitchens, this means the sink sits under or near the window. B&Q offers 600mm and 800mm sink base units — plan your plumbing space accordingly.

Appliance Integration

If you're buying integrated appliances from B&Q or elsewhere, make sure your base unit widths match. Standard integrated dishwashers, washing machines, and ovens are 600mm wide. Integrated fridges come in 600mm or 500mm widths. Mark these on your plan before finalising your unit order.

Corner Solutions

Corners are often the trickiest part of a kitchen layout. B&Q offers several corner unit solutions — traditional L-shaped corner units, magic corner pull-outs, and carousel fittings. Each requires a different base unit width on each adjoining side, typically 900mm or 1000mm. This is something you can clearly visualise using the drag-and-drop tools in our kitchen planner.
